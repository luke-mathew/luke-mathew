The Honeycomb Model, created in 2011, helps businesses understand how users engage with social media by breaking it down into seven key areas: Identity, Presence, Relationships, Reputation, Groups, Conversations, and Sharing. Each area focuses on different aspects of social media interaction, such as how users manage their privacy (Identity) or how content is shared (Sharing). By using this model, businesses can decide which aspects to focus on based on their goals, whether it's building a strong online presence, managing reputation, or fostering relationships. I learned that the model provides a flexible way to tailor social media strategies and improve engagement with the target audience.
